Apple Maps on the web launches in beta

July 24, 2024

On July 24, 2024, Apple launched a public beta of Apple Maps on the web, allowing global users to access Maps directly from their browsers. The web version provides driving and walking directions, place information, photos, hours, ratings, reviews, food ordering from place cards, and curated Guides for exploring cities. Additional features, like Look Around, will be added soon. Developers using MapKit JS can link to Maps on the web for detailed place information and directions. Currently available in English, it supports Safari and Chrome on Mac and iPad, and Chrome and Edge on Windows PCs, with plans to expand language, browser, and platform support over time. Availability varies by region.

Pixel 9’s ‘Add Me’ feature puts you in a group photo even when you’re not there

July 26, 2024

A new ad for the Pixel 9 has leaked, showcasing a feature called "Add Me," which ensures no one is left out of group photos. In the ad, a person uses the Pixel 9 to take a photo of friends, then swaps places with them to get her own picture. The Pixel 9’s display shows ghostly images of the friends beside her, and when the photo is taken, she appears in it as if she were always there. This feature stitches two images with the same background together. Other leaked features include Google’s Gemini assistant and "Pixel Screenshots," which uses AI to search screenshots, and an upgraded Magic Editor that may allow background replacement based on prompts. "Add Me" follows Google's trend of tools that blend reality, such as the Magic Eraser and Best Take, which modify images to enhance photo quality. The Pixel 9 is set to debut on August 13th.

How Google uses AI to reduce stop-and-go traffic on your route — and fight fuel emissions

Jul 29, 2024

At the start of 2020, a Google Research team was tasked with exploring ideas to accelerate climate mitigation. One evening, Dotan Emanuel, a software engineer on the team, discussed these ideas with his family, leading his wife to suggest addressing traffic light inefficiencies. This idea sparked the development of Project Green Light, which uses AI and Google Maps data to optimize traffic light timings, reducing stop-and-go traffic and emissions. The initiative leverages over a decade of Google Maps driving trends to create an AI model that recommends adjustments to traffic light timings. These recommendations, which cities can implement quickly without needing new hardware or software, aim to improve traffic flow and reduce emissions. Since its first pilot in 2021, Project Green Light has expanded to over 70 intersections across cities worldwide, including Rio de Janeiro, Seattle, Bengaluru, and Boston. The project has shown potential to reduce stops by up to 30% and emissions at intersections by up to 10%, benefiting up to 30 million car rides monthly. The team plans to scale the project to hundreds of cities and thousands of intersections in the coming years, aiming to enhance the driving experience and contribute to climate mitigation. “You know when you drive through a sequence of five green lights, and it feels like your lucky day?” Dotan says. “We want to make that feeling more common to more people around the world.”

Indie studio opens to share canceled Magic School project

July 29, 2024

Dinosaur Polo Club, the indie studio behind Mini Metro and Mini Motorways, has openly revealed the cancellation of their project, codenamed Magic School. The game, which aimed to expand the studio's scope, was abandoned due to the need for a much larger team. Despite this, the studio shared footage and concept images, highlighting the team's impressive work. This transparency is notable in the typically secretive game industry, and the studio chooses to celebrate the lessons learned and the team's efforts.

Arc’teryx’s new powered pants could make hikers feel 30 pounds lighter

July 29, 2024

Arc'teryx has teamed up with Skip, a Google X Labs spinoff, to create the MO/GO pants, exoskeleton suits designed to enhance hiking experiences. These powered pants feature lightweight electric motors at the knees that boost leg strength uphill and absorb impact downhill. Weighing around seven pounds with a three-hour rechargeable battery, the MO/GO pants use carbon fiber braces and are integrated into Arc'teryx Gamma hiking pants for easy wear. Marketed as a mobility device rather than a medical one, the pants can make the wearer feel up to 30 pounds lighter by adjusting assistance in real time based on gait and leg movements. Users can control the level of assistance with a button. Despite not being discreet, the MO/GO pants aim to make challenging hikes more accessible. They are priced at $5,000, with a preorder discount bringing the cost to $4,500 and an expected release in late 2025. Fast Company reported that the exoskeleton takes some time to adjust to. Skip and Arc'teryx also offer eight-hour rentals for $80 on select trails in the Western US and Canada for those wanting to test the pants first.

Qualcomm’s new Snapdragon chip aims to bring 5G to sub-$100 devices

July 30, 2024

Qualcomm announced the Snapdragon 4s Gen 2 chip, targeting 5G accessibility for 2.8 billion users in price-sensitive markets like India and Latin America. Manufacturers have struggled to attract feature phone buyers in India due to a lack of 5G models under $100. The entry-level segment in India declined by 14% year-on-year, while the overall market grew 11%, dominated by the $200-$400 segment. Qualcomm aims to address this gap with the 4s Gen 2 chip, which Xiaomi will use in its first device in India later this year. The Snapdragon 4s Gen 2, a cost-effective version of the Snapdragon 4 Gen 2, is built on 4nm technology with an octa-core Kryo CPU. It supports standalone 5G deployment, suitable for markets like India where Jio offers standalone 5G networks. However, it lacks support for non-standalone 5G, which is common in other regions. IDC's Kiranjeet Kaur notes that while India's push for low-cost 5G phones is strong, global momentum is slower. Qualcomm's cost-cutting measures for the Snapdragon 4s Gen 2 aim to support affordable 5G phones in emerging markets, potentially driving competition and momentum in the lower price segment.
